# Volunteer User Guide - Oakland Animal Services Portal

## Overview

This guide is for **volunteers** at Oakland Animal Services who help with animal care and observation. As a volunteer, you can view animal information, add notes about your observations, and help staff track animal welfare. Your account provides the access you need for fieldwork while maintaining appropriate security.

## Your Volunteer Account

### Account Access

Your volunteer account provides:
- View all animal information
- Scan QR codes at kennels
- Create medical observations
- Create behavioral notes
- See existing notes from staff and other volunteers

### Time-Limited Access

**Important**: Volunteer accounts **automatically expire** on a date set by administrators (typically end of volunteer term or training period).

**[SCREENSHOT: Login screen showing "Account expired" message]**

**What happens when your account expires**:
- You cannot log in after expiration date
- Your existing notes remain in the system
- All work you've done is preserved

**To extend access**:
- Contact your volunteer coordinator
- Administrator can update your expiration date
- You'll receive new access without losing any data

### First-Time Login

**[SCREENSHOT: Volunteer receiving welcome email]**

You'll receive an email with:
- **Username**: Your unique login
- **Temporary password**: Must be changed on first login
- **Expiration date**: When your access ends
- **Portal URL**: Where to access the system

**See [Getting Started Guide](./01-Getting-Started.md)** for detailed login instructions.

## Quick Start: Scanning Kennels

The fastest way to record observations is by scanning kennel QR codes with your phone.

### Step-by-Step Workflow

**[SCREENSHOT: Complete QR scanning workflow diagram]**

**Step 1: Access the Portal**

1. Open portal on your phone (bookmark it!)
2. Log in with your credentials
3. You'll see the home screen

**Step 2: Start QR Scanner**

**[SCREENSHOT: Home screen with "Scan QR Code" button]**

1. Tap **"Scan QR Code"** button
2. Grant camera permission if prompted (first time only)
3. Scanner activates with camera view

**Step 3: Scan Kennel QR Code**

**[SCREENSHOT: Phone scanning kennel QR code]**

1. Point camera at QR code on kennel door
2. Hold steady, 6-12 inches away
3. Code scans automatically when detected
4. Portal loads animal's profile instantly

**Step 4: Review Animal Info**

**[SCREENSHOT: Animal profile on mobile]**

You can now see:
- Animal's photo, name, and ID
- Age, breed, species
- Medical history
- Behavioral assessments
- Handler level and special needs

**Step 5: Add Your Observation**

**[SCREENSHOT: Create note buttons on mobile]**

- Tap **"+ New Note"** (Medical tab) for health observations
- Tap **"+ New Behavior Note"** (Behavior tab) for temperament observations

**Step 6: Move to Next Kennel**

After submitting your note:
- Tap **Back** button
- Tap **"Scan QR Code"** again
- Scan next kennel
- Repeat!

### Mobile Tips

**Add Portal to Home Screen**:
**[SCREENSHOT: Add to home screen process]**

- iPhone: Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen
- Android: Chrome → Menu → Add to Home Screen

Now you can open portal like a native app!

**Use Dark Mode in Dim Areas**:
- Tap your username (top right)
- Toggle dark mode on
- Easier on eyes in shelter lighting

**Keep Screen Awake**:
- Your phone may sleep during rounds
- Adjust screen timeout in phone settings
- Or keep tapping screen between kennels

## Adding Medical Observations

### When to Create a Medical Note

Create a medical observation when you notice:
- **Health changes**: Coughing, sneezing, limping, lethargy
- **Injuries**: Cuts, scrapes, swelling, wounds
- **Digestive issues**: Vomiting, diarrhea, loss of appetite
- **Behavioral changes** related to health: Sudden aggression (may indicate pain), hiding (may indicate illness)
- **Cleanliness issues**: Dirty kennel, soiled bedding (may indicate digestive problem)

### Creating a Medical Note

**[SCREENSHOT: Create medical note form on mobile]**

**Step 1: Open Medical Tab**

1. On animal's profile, tap **"Medical"** tab
2. Tap **"+ New Note"** button
3. Form appears

**Step 2: Fill Out Form**

**Case Title** (required):
- Short description: "Limping", "Sneezing", "Not Eating"
- Be specific but brief

**Observation** (required):
- Detailed description of what you noticed
- Include when, how often, how severe
- Mention any relevant context

**Status** (auto-set):
- New notes default to "Raised" (needs attention)
- You don't need to change this

**Step 3: Check for Duplicates**

**[SCREENSHOT: Similar notes warning on mobile]**

As you type, watch for **"Similar notes found"** section:
- Shows existing notes about the same issue
- Keywords are highlighted
- Helps you avoid duplicates

**If you see your observation already reported**:
- Skip creating duplicate
- Note has already been flagged for staff
- Move on to next kennel

**If your observation is different**:
- Create the note
- Your additional details help staff

**Step 4: Submit**

1. Review your note
2. Tap **"Submit"**
3. Note appears immediately
4. Staff are notified

### Example Medical Notes

**Good examples**:

**Example: Limping**
```
Dog is limping on left front leg. Noticed during morning walk at 9 AM. 
Limp is consistent - doesn't put full weight on leg. Not licking or 
favoring the paw, seems to be higher up. Dog is otherwise active and 
eating normally.
```

**Example: Eye Discharge**
```
Cat has yellowish discharge from right eye. Eye appears slightly 
squinted. Discharge is thick, not watery. First noticed during 
10 AM kennel check. Left eye looks normal.
```

**Example: Vomiting**
```
Found vomit in kennel during 2 PM check. Appears to be mostly 
undigested food. Dog seems normal otherwise - alert, tail wagging. 
Water bowl is empty (refilled). Will monitor.
```

**Poor examples** (too vague):

Bad: "Dog seems sick"
- What symptoms? When noticed?

Bad: "Something wrong with paw"
- Which paw? What's wrong specifically?

Bad: "Not acting right"
- What specific behaviors are different?

## Adding Behavioral Notes

### When to Create a Behavioral Note

Create a behavioral note when you observe:
- **Temperament**: Friendly, shy, playful, anxious, calm, energetic
- **Training level**: Responds to commands, leash manners, house-trained
- **Social behaviors**: How they interact with people, other animals
- **Handling notes**: Easy to handle, requires experienced handler, specific triggers
- **Play style**: Gentle, rough, fetch-oriented, toy-motivated
- **Adoption readiness**: What type of home would be best

### Creating a Behavioral Note

**[SCREENSHOT: Create behavior note form on mobile]**

**Step 1: Open Behavior Tab**

1. On animal's profile, tap **"Behavior"** tab
2. Scroll past compatibility boxes
3. Tap **"+ New Behavior Note"** button

**Step 2: Fill Out Form**

**Observation Title** (required):
- Brief summary: "Great with volunteers", "Reactive on leash", "Loves fetch"

**Behavioral Notes** (required):
- Detailed description of what you observed
- Include context (where, when, duration)
- Focus on specific behaviors, not assumptions

**Step 3: Submit**

1. Review your note
2. Tap **"Submit"**
3. Note appears in behavior section

### Example Behavioral Notes

**Good examples**:

**Example: Playful and Gentle**
```
Spent 30 minutes in play yard with dog. Very playful, loves fetch. 
Gentle taking treats from hand. Sat politely when asked (knows basic 
commands). Great with volunteers - tail wagging, friendly greeting. 
Would be excellent for active family. No jumping or mouthing.
```

**Example: Needs Experienced Handler**
```
Dog pulled strongly on leash during walk. Barked and lunged at two other 
dogs we passed (one small, one large). Calmed down after distance increased. 
Responded well to "sit" command when not distracted. Would need experienced 
handler comfortable with leash reactivity. Friendly with people.
```

**Example: Shy but Warming Up**
```
Cat was hiding in back of kennel when I arrived. Offered treats from 
distance - took 10 minutes before she approached. Once comfortable, 
allowed gentle petting and purred. Didn't hiss or swat. Would need 
patient adopter willing to give her time to adjust.
```

**Poor examples**:

Bad: "Good dog"
- Too vague, no specific observations

Bad: "Aggressive"
- What specific behaviors? Toward whom/what?

Bad: "Would make great pet"
- This is opinion, not observation

## What You Can and Cannot Do

### You CAN:

**View Information**:
- All animal profiles
- All medical observations (yours and others)
- All behavioral notes (yours and others)
- Handler level and compatibility info
- Basic animal details (age, breed, etc.)

**Create Notes**:
- Medical observations when you notice health concerns
- Behavioral notes when you observe temperament/training

**Use Portal Features**:
- QR code scanning
- Manual search by kennel location
- Dark mode toggle
- View your profile

### You CANNOT:

**Edit or Delete**:
- Cannot edit notes after submission
- Cannot delete your notes
- Cannot change note status (Raised/Resolved)
- Cannot edit other people's notes

**Administrative Functions**:
- Cannot create other user accounts
- Cannot change your expiration date
- Cannot access full activity logs
- Cannot manage kennel locations
- Cannot see auth events in activity log

**Note Management**:
- Cannot mark observations as "Resolved"
- Cannot assign notes to specific staff
- Cannot change handler level colors
- Cannot edit animal profiles

### Why These Restrictions?

**Data Integrity**: Preventing note editing preserves original observations for medical/legal records

**Accountability**: All notes are timestamped and attributed to specific users

**Security**: Limiting admin functions prevents accidental changes to critical settings

**Professional Workflow**: Only veterinary staff should resolve medical concerns

## Best Practices for Volunteers

### Before Your Shift

**[SCREENSHOT: Checklist graphic]**

**Check Your Equipment**:
- Phone fully charged (or bring charger)
- Portal bookmarked on phone
- Added to home screen for quick access
- Camera permissions granted

**Review Recent Notes**:
- Check home screen for animals you'll see
- Read any urgent updates from staff
- Note any special handling requirements

**Know Your Focus Areas**:
- Which kennels will you check today?
- Any specific animals needing extra observation?
- Special tasks assigned by coordinator?

### During Rounds

**[SCREENSHOT: Volunteer making rounds with phone]**

**Be Systematic**:
- Start at one end, work to the other
- Don't skip kennels
- Use QR codes for speed
- Note even "all normal" observations mentally

**Observe Thoroughly**:
- Take time to really look at each animal
- Notice changes from previous visits
- Check food/water bowls
- Look for injuries, illness signs
- Watch behavior and energy level

**Document Immediately**:
- Don't wait until end of shift
- Note observations while fresh
- Use voice-to-text if typing is slow
- Keep notes brief but specific

**When in Doubt, Report**:
- Better to over-report than under-report
- Staff can triage priority
- You're the eyes and ears for animals
- No observation is "too small"

### Writing Quality Notes

**Focus on Facts, Not Opinions**:
- Bad: "This dog is mean"
- Good: "Dog growled when I approached kennel"

**Include Context**:
- Bad: "Acting weird"
- Good: "Pacing in circles, didn't greet me like usual"

**Be Specific About Timing**:
- Bad: "Recently"
- Good: "During my 10 AM round today"

**Describe Severity**:
- Bad: "Coughing"
- Good: "Occasional dry cough, 2-3 times in 5 minutes"

**Note What's Normal Too**:
- Bad: "Dog is fine"
- Good: "Dog eating, drinking, and playing normally"

### Safety First

**[SCREENSHOT: Handler level indicator colors]**

**Always check handler level**:
- Green: Safe for all volunteers
- Yellow: Experienced volunteers only
- Blue: Specialized training required
- Pink: Staff only, do not handle

**If you're unsure**:
- Ask staff before handling
- Read behavioral notes first
- Start with cautious approach
- Never handle animals above your training level

**Report safety concerns immediately**:
- Aggressive behavior
- Escape attempts
- Kennel damage
- Anything that could harm animals or people

### Avoiding Duplicate Notes

**Before creating a note**:
1. Check Medical tab for recent notes
2. Search for keywords related to your observation
3. Watch for "similar notes" while typing
4. Ask: "Is this adding new information?"

**If someone already reported the issue**:
- Don't create duplicate
- Issue is already flagged for staff
- Your observation confirms theirs (which is good!)
- Move on to next kennel

**When to create a note anyway**:
- You have different details to add
- Symptom has changed or progressed
- Issue affects different body part
- Your timing adds context ("Still limping 3 days later")

## Common Volunteer Questions

**Q: What if I forget to add a note right away?**
A: You can add notes later from the home screen. Search for the animal by kennel location or ID, then add your observation. Include timing context: "During morning rounds at 9 AM..."

**Q: Can I see who else has reported issues?**
A: Yes! All notes show the author's name and timestamp. This helps you see what others have observed.

**Q: What happens to my notes when my account expires?**
A: Your notes remain in the system permanently and continue to help staff care for the animals. Only your login access expires.

**Q: Should I report if an animal seems "normal"?**
A: You don't need to create notes for normal observations. However, if an animal was previously sick and now seems recovered, that's worth noting!

**Q: Can I take photos of animals?**
A: Photo attachments aren't currently supported in the portal. Take photos with your phone camera if needed, and include description in your note.

**Q: What if I make a mistake in a note?**
A: Contact staff immediately. They can edit or delete notes. Include what needs to be corrected.

**Q: How do I know if my note was seen?**
A: All notes are visible to staff immediately. Urgent issues are triaged by veterinary team. You don't get individual notification, but staff see all observations.

**Q: Can I see resolved medical issues?**
A: Yes! Resolved notes stay visible in the Medical tab, usually with a green indicator or separate section.

**Q: What if QR code won't scan?**
A: Use manual search on home screen. Type the kennel location (e.g., "Dog E:5") and search. If QR code is damaged, report to staff for replacement.

**Q: Should I report duplicate observations by other animals?**
A: No need to report about different animals (e.g., "Both dogs in Cat W:5 are sneezing"). Create one note per animal with their specific symptoms.

## Troubleshooting

**Can't log in**:
- Check username/password spelling
- Verify account hasn't expired
- Clear browser cache
- Contact volunteer coordinator

**QR scanner not working**:
- Grant camera permissions in browser
- Try different angle/distance
- Ensure good lighting
- Use manual search as backup

**Note won't submit**:
- Check all required fields filled
- Ensure internet connection
- Try refreshing page
- Contact staff if persistent

**Can't find animal I saw**:
- Verify kennel location spelling
- Try searching by animal name
- Check if animal was transferred
- Ask staff for assistance
